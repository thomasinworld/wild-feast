## How to Use These Maps

### Planning Your Trip

1. **Click the location links** above to open in Google Maps
2. **Check the "Nearby" feature** in Google Maps for trails and access points
3. **Use satellite view** to identify forest types and terrain
4. **Check current conditions** via:
   - [Washington Trails Association](https://www.wta.org/)
   - [Oregon Hikers](https://www.oregonhikers.org/)
   - Forest Service websites

### Important Reminders

- **Get required permits** before visiting:
  - [Mount Rainier NP](https://www.nps.gov/mora/) - Free personal use permit for mushrooms (1 gallon/day limit)
  - [Gifford Pinchot NF](https://www.fs.usda.gov/giffordpinchot) - Free personal use permit
  - [Olympic NF](https://www.fs.usda.gov/olympic) - Check current regulations

- **Check closures**: Some areas may be closed due to:
  - Fire danger
  - Restoration efforts
  - Wildlife protection
  - Seasonal conditions

- **Respect private land**: Many areas near forests are private‚Äîalways get permission

## Creating Your Own Foraging Map

### Using Google My Maps

You can create your own custom foraging map:

1. Go to [Google My Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/d/)
2. Click "Create a New Map"
3. Add markers for your discoveries:
   - üçÑ Red pins: Mushroom spots
   - ü´ê Blue pins: Berry patches
   - üåø Green pins: Wild greens
4. Add notes about:
   - Date found
   - Abundance
   - Best access route
   - Seasonal notes

### Privacy Note

**Keep your best spots secret!** Many foragers maintain private maps of their productive patches. Consider:
- Not sharing exact GPS coordinates publicly
- Sharing general areas instead ("Mount Rainier area" vs. specific GPS)
- Building your own library of spots over years
